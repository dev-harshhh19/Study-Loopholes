Practical 1: Multimedia Hardware & Tools

Aim

To study the multimedia hardware system and basic software tools.

Background Information

A multimedia system is capable of processing, storing, and presenting various types of media such as text, graphics, audio, and video. Hardware components like high-speed CPUs, sufficient RAM, dedicated graphics cards, and input/output devices (microphones, speakers, scanners) are essential. Software tools include authoring tools, image editors (Photoshop), audio editors (Audacity), and video editors (Premiere Pro) that enable the creation and manipulation of multimedia content.

Study Details

  1. Input Devices

    Study various devices used to capture media: Digital Cameras, Scanners, Microphones, MIDI keyboards, and Graphic Tablets.

  2. Output Devices

    Identify devices used for presentation: High-resolution Monitors, Data Projectors, Speakers, and Printers.

  3. Storage & Processing

    Understand the role of RAM, SSDs/HDDs, and Multi-core Processors in handling large multimedia files and rendering tasks.

  4. Software Categories

    Graphic Tools: Adobe Photoshop, CorelDRAW, GIMP.
    Audio Tools: Audacity, Adobe Audition, FL Studio.
    Video Tools: Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve.
    Authoring Tools: Adobe Animate, Director, ToolBook.
