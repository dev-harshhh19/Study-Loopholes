Practical 12: Mobile Video Clip Editing

Aim

To shoot a short video clip using a mobile phone and edit it by adding transitions, titles, and background audio.

Background Information

Modern mobile video editing apps like VN Editor, CapCut, or LumaFusion allow users to perform complex editing tasks on their smartphones. Video editing includes sequencing clips, adding transitions to smooth movements between clips, and overlaying titles and background music for a professional finish.

Procedure

  1. Shooting the Video

    Use your mobile phone to shoot a 1-minute video clip in Landscape (16:9) or Portrait (9:16) at 1080p resolution. Ensure good lighting and stable hand movements.

  2. Importing & Sequencing

    Open the video editing app and import the recorded clip. Trim unnecessary parts from the beginning or end using the Split/Trim tool.

  3. Adding Transitions

    If using multiple clips, click the '+' icon between them and select a transition like 'Dissolve', 'Zoom', or 'Slide'. This ensures the video flows naturally.

  4. Inserting Titles & Text

    Intro Title: Add a text overlay at the beginning with your project title.
    Subtitles: Add lower-thirds or captions where necessary to explain parts of the video.

  5. Background Audio Overlay

    Go to the Audio track → Music. Choose a royalty-free background track. Ensure the background volume is lower than the original narration (around 10-15%).

  6. Exporting the Project

    Export the final video at 1080p, 30 FPS. Check the final output for any audio-visual synchronization issues.
