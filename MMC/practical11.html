Practical 11: Audio Recording & Editing

Aim

To record and edit an audio narration by applying trimming, noise-reduction, and fade effects.

Background Information

Audio editing involves manipulating digital sound files to improve quality or create effects. Tools like Audacity allow users to visualize sound waves and perform non-destructive editing. Essential processes include Trimming (removing unwanted silence), Noise Reduction (cleaning background hiss), and Fading (smooth transitions at start/end).

Procedure (Using Audacity)

  1. Recording Audio

    Connect a microphone. Press the Record (Red) button. Speak clearly into the mic and record a short 30-second narration. Press Stop (Yellow Square) when finished.

  2. Trimming Silence

    Select the unwanted silent parts at the beginning or end of the waveform. Press the Delete key or Ctrl+L to silence selected sections.

  3. Noise Reduction

    Noise Profile: Select a small part of the waveform where only background noise exists.
    Apply: Go to Effect → Noise Removal and Repair → Noise Reduction. Click 'Get Noise Profile', then select the whole track and click 'OK' to apply.

  4. Applying Fade Effects

    Select the first 2 seconds of the audio → Effect → Fading → Fade In. Select the last 2 seconds → Effect → Fading → Fade Out.

  5. Final Export

    Go to File → Export → Export as MP3. Set the bitrate to 192kbps or higher for better quality.
