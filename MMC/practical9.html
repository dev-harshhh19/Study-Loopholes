Practical 9: Image Tonality Correction

Aim

Design a scenery showing correction of image tonality.

Background Information

Tonality refers to the range of tones (light and dark) in an image. Correction techniques like Levels, Curves, and Brightness/Contrast are used to balance the highlights, midtones, and shadows. This ensures that the image has good contrast and does not look 'washed out' or 'too dark'.

Procedure

  1. Selecting a Scenery

    Open a scenery image that is either too bright or too dark. A photo of a forest or sunset works best.

  2. Using Levels (Ctrl+L)

    Go to Image → Adjustments → Levels. Adjust the sliders to ensure the histogram covers the full range from black to white.

  3. Fine-Tuning with Curves (Ctrl+M)

    Use the Curves tool to create an 'S' curve. This increases contrast by darkening shadows and brightening highlights simultaneously.

  4. Dodge & Burn

    Dodge Tool: Use it to brighten specific areas like sunlight reflecting on water.
    Burn Tool: Use it to darken shadows under trees for a more dramatic look.

  5. Saturation & Vibrance

    Adjust the Vibrance (Image → Adjustments → Vibrance) to make the colors of the scenery pop without making them look unnatural.

  6. Comparison

    Turn the adjustment layers on and off to see the drastic improvement in image depth and clarity.
